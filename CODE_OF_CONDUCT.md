# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want TrendPilot AI to be a practical, respectful, and safety-conscious open-source project. Contributors and users are expected to communicate professionally and help keep the project useful, lawful, and accessible.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of positive behavior include:

- Being respectful and constructive in discussions.
- Giving clear feedback without personal attacks.
- Respecting different experience levels and backgrounds.
- Explaining risks, limitations, and assumptions honestly.
- Keeping contributions aligned with public-data, license-safe, human-reviewed workflows.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory comments.
- Sharing private information without permission.
- Submitting credentials, tokens, cookies, or private datasets.
- Encouraging spam, fake engagement, platform abuse, or unlawful data collection.
- Promoting guaranteed-profit, auto-money, or deceptive automation claims.
- Repeatedly ignoring project safety and compliance boundaries.

## Project Safety Boundaries

TrendPilot AI is designed for public, lawful, human-reviewed trend intelligence workflows. It is not intended for spam automation, credential misuse, private data scraping, or guaranteed income schemes.

Contributors should help preserve those boundaries in issues, pull requests, examples, prompts, and workflows.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject pull requests, or restrict participation when behavior is harmful, abusive, off-topic, or inconsistent with this Code of Conduct.

If you notice a problem, report it respectfully with enough context for maintainers to review it.
